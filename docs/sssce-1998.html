DELTA INTELLECT

SSSCE 1998

Question 1:

  1. Consider the following statements

    \(S:\) All students with measles stay in the sick bay.

    \(T:\) All students in the sick bay do not do homework.

    Which of the following is/are valid deductions from the two statements?

    1. Kofi does not have measles so Kofi does his homework.
    2. George has done his homework therefore he does not stay in the sick bay.
    3. jane does not have measles so she does not stay in the sick bay.

  2. Solve the equation \(243_x =73\) where \(x\) is the base of the number.

Question 2:

  1. Simplify \(\displaystyle\left(\displaystyle\frac{27}{64}\right)^{-\frac{2}{3}}\).
  2. Solve the inequality \(4x+3 \leq 3(2x-1)\). Illustrate your answer on the number line.

Question 3:

  1. If \(C=216k\), find the least integral value of \(k\) that will make \(C\) a perfect square.

  2. In the diagram, \(WXYZ\) is a circle with centre \(O\). \(XZ\) and \(WY\) intersect at \(V\). \(\angle XOY = 110^\circ\) and \(\angle YVZ=100^\circ\). Calculate
    1. \(\angle XZY\)
    2. \(\angle WXZ\)

Question 4:

If \(t=\sqrt{\displaystyle\frac{p-r}{p+r}}\), find

  1. \(r\) in terms of \(p\) and \(t\).
  2. the value of \(r\), when \(t=3\) and \(p=10\).

Question 5:

The diagram above represents a rectangular compound 20 m by 18 m with a semi-circular portion cut off. Calculate

  1. the perimeter of the compound
  2. the area of the compound. \(\left[\text{Take } \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right]\)

Question 6:

  1. If \(3x-4=2(y-2)\), find \(\displaystyle\frac{x}{y}\), where \(y \neq 0\).
  2. Use logarithm tables to evaluate \(\displaystyle\frac{86.19 \times (0.0462)^2}{\sqrt{0.846}}\)

Question 7:

The diagram above shows a circle \(PQRS\) with centre \(C\). Quadrilateral \(CPSR\) is a rhombus \(\angle QPC = \angle CRQ = x\) and \(\angle PQR = q\).

  1. Find
    1. \(q\)
    2. \(x\)
    3. \(\angle QRS\)
  2. Show that triangle \(PQR\) is an equilateral triangle.

Question 9:

  1. Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only, construct
    1. a quadrilateral \(ABCD\), where \(|AB|=8\) cm, \(|AD|=6\) cm, \(|BC|=10\) cm, \(\angle BAD=60^\circ\) and \(\angle ADC=135^\circ\);
    2. the locus, \(l_1\) of points equidistant from \(BC\) and \(CD\);
    3. the line, \(l_2\), from \(B\) perpendicular to \(l_1\).
    1. Locate \(E\), the point of intersection of \(l_1\) and \(l_2\).
    2. Measure \(|AE|\) and \(|DE|\).

Question 10:

  1. Copy and complete the following table of values for the relation \(y=10+6x-3x^2\) for \(-3 \leq x \leq 5\).
\(x\) \(-3\) \(-2\) \(-1\) \(0\) \(1\) \(2\) \(3\) \(4\) \(5\)
\(y\) \(10\) \(13\) \(1\) \(-14\)

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 1 unit n the \(x\)-axis and 2 cm to 5 units on the \(y\)-axis, draw the graph of the relation for the given interval.

  2. From the graph, solve the equation
    1. \(10+6x-3x^2=0\)
    2. \(5+2x-x^2=0\)

  3. Find the equation of the axis of symmetry.

Question 11:

  1. A ship sails due north from a point \(P\) to a point \(Q\), 4 km away. It then sails on a bearing of \(090^\circ\) to a point \(R\), 3 km from \(Q\). Find the distance between \(P\) and \(R\).

  2. Two points \(A\) and \(C\), on opposite sides of a vertical pole, are on the same level ground as the foot of the pole, \(B\). The angles of elevation of the top of the pole \(D\) from \(A\) and \(C\) are \(30^\circ\) and \(48^\circ\) respectively. If the distance between \(A\) and \(C\) is 50 m, find \(|BD|\), the height of the pole.
