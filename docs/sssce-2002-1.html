DELTA INTELLECT

SSSCE 2002

Question 1:

  1. Without using calculators, simplify \(\displaystyle\frac{\frac{3}{4} (3\frac{3}{8} + 1\frac{5}{6})}{2 \frac{1}{8}-1\frac{1}{2}}\).
  2. Find the truth set of the inequality \(7x+4 < \displaystyle\frac{1}{2}(4x+3)\). Illustrate your answer on the number line.

Question 2:

  1. Consider the following statements:

    \(P:\) If students word hard, they will pass their examination.

    \(Q:\) If students pass their examinations then they do not sleep in class.

  2. Draw a Venn diagram to represent the statement \(P\) and \(Q\).
  3. Deduce whether the following conclusions are valid or not.
    1. Nana does not sleep in class so she is hard working.
    2. Nii does not pass his examination so he sleeps in class.
    3. Naa works hard so she does not sleep in class.

Question 3:

  1. \((3.46)^2 - (1.54)^2 = 10x\), find the value of \(x\).
  2. Find the truth set of the equation \(5x^2=(x+2)(x+3)\).

Question 4:

Tickets for a film show were sold at 4,500.00 cedis to the general public and 3,750.00 cedis to students. 400 people attended the show and 1,680,000.00 cedis was collected in ticket sales.

  1. How many tickets were sold to students?
  2. Mr. Mensah was issued with 25 tickets to be sold to the general public and 20 tickets to be sold to students. How much did Mr. Mensah collect after selling all the tickets issued to him?

Question 5:

  1. using a ruler and a pair of compasses only, construct
    1. triangle \(ABC\) with \(|AB|=10\) cm, \(\angle ABC=30^\circ\) and \(\angle BAC=45^\circ\).
    2. a perpendicular from \(C\) to \(AB\) at \(D\).
  2. Measure \(|CD|\).

Question 6:

A woman bought 130 kg of tomatoes for 52,000.00 cedis. She sold half of them at a profit of \(30\%\). The rest of the tomatoes began to go bad, she then reduced the selling price per kg by \(12\%\). Calculate

  1. the new selling price per kg:
  2. the percentage profit on the whole transaction if she threw away 5 kg of bad tomatoes.

Question 7:

  1. The points \(P,Q, R\) and \(S\) are vertices of a parallelogram in the cartesian plane. The coordinates of \(P\) and \(R\) are \((-8, 2)\) and \((5, -2)\) respectively and \(\overrightarrow{QR}=\begin{pmatrix} 3\\-4 \end{pmatrix}\). Find
    1. the coordinates of \(Q\) and \(S\).
    2. the magnitude of \(\overrightarrow{PR}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\frac{1}{u} + \displaystyle\frac{1}{v} = \displaystyle\frac{1}{f}\) and \(d=u+v\)
    1. Express \(d\) i terms of \(f\) and \(u\).
    2. Find the value of \(d\), when \(f=6\) and \(u=6.5\)

Question 8:

  1. Copy and complete the following table for the relation \(y=7+4x-3x^2\) for the interval \(-3 \leq x \leq 4\).
\(x\) \(-3\) \(-2\) \(-1\) \(0\) \(0.5\) \(1\) \(1.5\) \(2\) \(2.5\) \(3\) \(3.5\) \(4\)
\(y\) \(-32\) \(7\) \(8.25\) \(3\) \(-8\) \(-15.5\)
  1. Taking 2 cm as 1 unit on the \(x\)-axis and 2 cm as 5 units on the \(y\)-axis, draw the graph of \(y=7+4x-3x^2\) for the given interval.
  2. Draw on the same graph sheet, the graph of the relation \(y+2x+2=0\).
  3. Using your graphs,
    1. solve the equation \(9+6x-3x62=0\)
    2. find the values of \(x\) for which \(17+4x-3x^2=0\).

Question 9:

The table below shows the frequency distribution of marks scored by 80 candidates in an examination.

Marks\((\%)\) \(0-9\) \(10-19\) \(20-29\) \(30-39\) \(40-49\) \(50-59\) \(60-69\) \(70-79\) \(80-89\) \(90-99\)
Frequency 2 5 8 18 20 15 5 4 2 1
  1. Draw a cumulative frequency curve for the distribution.
  2. Use your graph to estimate
    1. the median mark
    2. the \(60^\text{th}\) percentile mark
  3. If the mark for distinction was \(70\%\), what percentage of students passed with distinction?

Question 10:

  1. A chord \(PQ\) of a circle of radius 5 cm subtends an angle of \(70^\circ\) at the centre, \(O\). Find correct to 3 significant figures,
    1. the length of the chord \(PQ\);
    2. the length of the arc \(PQ\);
    3. the area of the sector \(POQ\);
    4. the area of the minor segment cut off by \(PQ\).
  2. A right circular cone has base radius 5 cm and height 12 cm. Calculate
    1. its volume;
    2. its total surface area. \(\left[ \text{Take } \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right]\)

Question 11:

  1. the sum of two numbers is 8 and their product is \(-33\). Find the numbers.
  2. Two given points \(S\) and \(T\) which are at opposite sides of a tower are 20 m apart. \(S,T\) and the foot of the tower are in the same straight line. The angle of elevation of the top of the tower from \(S\) and \(T\) are \(32^\circ\) and \(45^\circ\) respectively. Find, correct to one decimal place, the height of the tower.
  3. A clerk spends \(\frac{1}{5},\, \frac{1}{3}\) and \(\frac{1}{8}\) of his salary on rent, transport and entertainment respectively. If after all these expenses he had 820.00 cedis left, find how much he earns per annum.

Question 12:

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on each axis, draw on a sheet of graph paper, two perpendicular axes \(Ox\) and \(Oy\) for the intervals \(-10 \leq x \leq 10\) and \(-10 \leq y \leq 10\).
  2. Draw on this graph sheet, indicating the coordinates of all the vertices.
    1. \(\bigtriangleup UVW\) with \(U(3, 7), V(6, 7)\) and \(W(3, 3)\).
    2. the image \(\bigtriangleup U_1 V_1 W_1\) of triangle \(\bigtriangleup UVW\) under a reflection in the \(x\)-axis where \(U \to U_1, V \to V_1\) and \(W \to W_1\).
    3. the image \(\bigtriangleup U_2 V_2 W_2\) of triangle \(\bigtriangleup U_1 V_1 W_1\) under the mapping \(\begin{pmatrix} x\\y \end{pmatrix} \to \begin{pmatrix} x+y\\x \end{pmatrix}\) where \(U_1 \to U_2, V_1 \to V_2\) and \(W_1 \to W_2\).
    1. Find the equation of the line joining points \(V_1\) and \(U\).
    2. Calculate \(|WW_1|\).
