DELTA INTELLECT

SSSCE 2000

Question 1:

The set \(P=\{ \text{multiples of 3} \}, Q=\{ \text{factors of 72} \}\) and \(R=\{ \text{even numbers} \}\) are the subsets of \(U=\{ 18 \leq x \leq 36 \}\).

  1. List the elements of \(P,Q\) and \(R\)
  2. Find
    1. \(P \cap Q\)
    2. \(Q \cap R\)
    3. \(P \cap R\)
  3. What is the relationship between \(P \cap Q\) and \(Q \cap R\).

Question 2:

  1. Find \(R\), if \(5R^2+(22.55)^2=(27.45)^2\).
  2. Evaluate \(\sqrt{1,500}+3\sqrt{3} \times 5\sqrt{5}+2\sqrt{15}(\sqrt{15} - 3)\) in the form \(p+q\sqrt{15}\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are integers.

Question 3:

  1. Solve the equation \(\log (5x-4)=\log (x+1)+\log 4\)

  2. In the diagram above, \(A,B,C\) and \(D\) are points on a circle. \(|AB|=|AC|, \angle BDC=50^\circ\) and \(\angle ABD=30^\circ\). Calculate the value of \(\angle CAD\).

Question 4:

Given that \(T=\displaystyle\frac{27V}{6V-5.4M}\).

  1. Make \(V\) the subject of the relation.
  2. If \(T=4.5\) and \(M=7\times 10^7\), calculate the value of \(V\), leaving your answer in standard form.

Question 5:

The table below shows the number of cars sold by a company from January to June 1990.

January February March April May June
7,100 7,668 10,366 9,940 8,236 7,810
  1. Draw a pie chart to illustrate the information.
  2. What is the percentage of cars sold in February?

Question 6:

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on both axes, draw on a sheet of graph paper two perpendicular axes \(Ox\) and \(Oy\), for the intervals \(-8 \leq x \leq 8\) and \(-8 \leq y \leq 12\).

  2. draw on the same graph sheet indicating clearly the coordinates of all vertices.
    1. the square \(PQRS\) with coordinates \(P(2,2), Q(6,2), R(6,6)\) and \(S(2,6)\).
    2. the image \(P_1 Q_1 R_1 S_1\) of the square \(PQRS\) under a reflection in the \(y\)-axis where \(P \to P_1, Q \to Q_1, R \to R_1\) and \(S \to S_1\).
    3. the image \(P_2 Q_2 R_2 S_2\) of the square \(PQRS\) under a translation by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} 4\\-8 \end{pmatrix}\) where \(P \to P_2, Q \to Q_2, R \to R_2\) and \(S \to S_2\).
    4. the image \(P_3 Q_3 R_3 S_3\) of the square \(PQRS\) under a rotation through \(180^\circ\) about the origin where \(P \to P_3, Q \to Q_3, R \to R_3\) and \(S \to S_3\).

  3. Find the vector \(\overrightarrow{P_2 P_3}\).

Question 7:

  1. The simple interest on 600,000.00 cedis for \(3 \frac{3}{4}\) years is 56,250.00 cedis. Find the rate percent per annum.
  2. Mr. Amos bought a car for 2.5 million cedis in 1985. He paid \(40\%\) of the cost and paid the rest in equal monthly installments. He took 8 years to make full payment for the car. Interest was at \(18\%\) simple interest. calculate
    1. the monthly installment.
    2. the total amount he paid for the car.
    3. the percentage increase in the cost of the car.

Question 8:

  1. The cost \(C\) of maintaining a household is partly constant and partly varies as the number of people \(n\), in the house. For 8 people, the cost is 70,000 cedis and for 10 people, the cost is 90,000 cedis. Find
    1. an expression for \(C\) in terms of \(n\).
    2. the weekly cost for 12 people
  2. Two passengers trains, A and B, 300 km apart, starts towards each other at the same time. They meet after 2 hours. If train B travels \(\frac{8}{7}\) as fast as train A, what is the speed of each train?

Question 9:

Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only,

  1. Construct
    1. triangle \(ABC\) such that \(|AB|=8\) cm, \(\angle BAC=105^\circ\) and \(\angle ABC=30^\circ\).
    2. the locus \(l_1\) of points equidistant from \(A\) and \(B\).
    3. the locus \(l_2\) of points equidistant from \(B\) and \(C\).
  2. Locate \(P\), the point of intersection of \(l_1\) and \(l_2\).
  3. Using \(PC\) as radius, draw a circle.
  4. Measure
    1. \(|BC|\)
    2. the radius of the circle.

Question 10:

  1. The annual rent of a rectangular plot of land is 1,024,000.00 cedis at a rate of 320.00 cedis per square metre. If the length is 80 m, find the width of the plot.

  2. A flower bed is in the form of a rectangle with semi-circular ends. The straight sides are 25 m long and the flower bes is 14 m wide.
    1. Find the area of the flower bed.
    2. If the cost of black soil is 955.75 cedis per square metre and the cost of labour is 800.00 cedis per square metre, find the cost of covering the bed with black soil. \(\left[\text{Take } \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right]\)

Question 11:

The data below show the distribution of marks obtained by 50 students in a test.

10 73 19 78 24 34 34 35 78 35
37 59 41 63 41 65 45 55 55 58
48 49 49 53 53 55 45 55 65 67
58 38 59 43 61 44 48 68 79 83
70 14 74 29 76 31 48 68 85 90

  1. Using class intervals of 10 - 19, 20 - 29 etc. construct frequency table for the distribution.

    1. Draw a histogram for the distribution.
    2. Use your histogram to estimate the mode.

  3. Calculate the mean mark of the distribution.

Question 12:

  1. Four angles of a hexagon are \(130^\circ, 160^\circ, 112^\circ\) and \(80^\circ\). If the remaining angles are equal, find the size of each of them.

  2. A ship sails from a point A in a direction \(065^\circ\) to a point B, 24 km away. From B, the ship sails 18 km due east to a point C. From C, the ship then sails 30 km due north to a point D. Calculate the bearing of D from A.
