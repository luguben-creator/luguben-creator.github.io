SSSCE 2001

Question 1:

  1. If \(P=\{ 1,2,3,4 \}\), write down all the subsets of \(P\) which are exactly two elements.
  2. \(A=\{ \text{Prime numbers less than 15} \}\), \(B=\{ \text{Even numbers less than 15} \}\) and \(C=\{ x: 3 \leq x < 12, \text{where} x \text{is an integer} \}\). List the elements of
    1. \(A \cap C\)
    2. \(B \cap C\)
    3. \((A \cup B)^\prime C\)

Question 2:

  1. If \(\sqrt{7}=2.646\), find without using calculator, the value of \(\sqrt{0.0007}\).
  2. Copy and complete the table below for addition base eight.
\(\oplus\) 1 3 5 7
1 2 4 6
3 6 10 12
5 6
7 12 14 16

Use your table to solve the following:

  1. \(3 \oplus x = 12\)
  2. \(n \oplus n =6\)

Question 3:

  1. If \(x^5=1024\), find the value of \(x\).
  2. Given that \(\log_{10} 7=0.8451\) and \(\log_{10} 3=0.4771\), find without using calculator, \(\log_{10} \left( \displaystyle\frac{9}{7} \right)\).

Question 4:

  1. Express \(441_\text{five}\) as a number in base four.
  2. Solve the inequality $x - (2+x) x + $

Question 5:

A group of 300 Mathematics teachers were classified as follows:

University Graduates \(120\)
Diplomats \(90\)
Specialist \(50\)
Others \(y\)
  1. Calculate the value of \(y\).
  2. Draw a pie chart to represent the above information.

Question 6:

  1. \(S=\displaystyle\frac{n}{2}[2a+(n-1)d]\), where \(n>0.\) Find the value of \(n\), if \(a=3, d=4\) and \(S=210\).
  2. Tickets for a film show were sold at 4,000 cedis per adult and 2,000 cedis per child. the total amount raised from the 500 tickets sold was 1,600,000 cedis.
    1. Find the number of \(\alpha\)) Adults who bought tickets for the show. \(\beta\)) Children who bought tickets for the show.
    2. \(12\%\) of the amount raised was paid as taxed and \(2\%\) of the amount raised was paid to the tickets sellers. Find the amount paid \(\alpha\)) as tax \(\beta\)) to the tickets sellers

Question 7:

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on both axes, draw on a sheet of graph paper two perpendicular axes \(Ox\) and \(Oy\) for the interval \(-10 \leq x \leq 10\) and \(-12 \leq y \leq 12\).

  2. Draw on the same graph sheet, showing clearly the coordinates of all vertices,
    1. the triangle \(PQR\) with \(P(4, 8),\overrightarrow{QP} = \begin{pmatrix} -2\\2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\overrightarrow{RQ} = \begin{pmatrix} 2\\4 \end{pmatrix}\).
    2. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_1 Q_1 R_1\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under a reflection in the line \(y=-2\), where \(P \to P_1, Q \to Q_1\) and \(R \to R_1\).
    3. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_2 Q_2 R_2\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under a translation by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} -8\\2 \end{pmatrix}\) where \(P \to P_2, Q \to Q_2\) and \(R \to R_2\).
    4. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_2 Q_2 R_2\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under a rotation through \(180^\circ\) about the origin.

  3. Find \(Q_2 Q_3\)

Question 8:

In the diagram, \(C\) is the centre of the circle \(PQRS, STQ\) and \(PCQ\) are straight lines and \(RS\) is parallel to \(QP\). Angle \(SPC=60^\circ\).

  1. Show that triangles \(CQT\) and \(RST\) are similar.
  2. Find
    1. \(\angle RSQ\)
    2. \(\angle CRQ\)

Question 9:

  1. Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only, construct
    1. \(\bigtriangleup GBC\) with \(\angle GBC=30^\circ, |BG|=12\) cm.
    2. \(l_1\), the locus of points 6 cm from C.
    3. \(l_2\), the perpendicular from \(C\) to \(BG\).
    1. Locate \(A\) and \(D\), the intersection of \(l_1\) and \(BG\).
    2. Measure \(|AD|\) and \(\angle ACD\).
    3. Calculate, correct to two significant figures, the area of the minor sector \(ACD\). [Take \(\pi =\) 3.142]

Question 10:

The marks obtained in a test by 40 pupils are as follows:

78 60 78 66 33 81 67 84 72 60
54 42 27 33 24 66 27 63 18 30
39 44 30 30 33 45 39 33 30 45
27 36 42 18 42 36 60 72 72 63
  1. Construct a frequency table using the class intervals 10-19, 20-29, 30-39 etc.
  2. Draw a histogram for the distribution taking 2 cm for the width of each bar and 2 cm to represent 2 pupils.
    1. Use your histogram to estimate the mode.
    2. Calculate the mean of the distribution.

Question 11:

The following diagram represent a field with a circular pond of diameter 14 m. \(PTS\) and \(QUR\) are semi-circles. \(PQRS\) is a rectangle with \(|PQ|=112\) m and \(|QR|=56\) m. Find:

  1. the distance round the field.
  2. the area of the field excluding the pond. \(\left[ \text{Take } \pi=\frac{22}{7} \right]\)

Question 12:

  1. If \(\sin \theta = \displaystyle\frac{3}{5}\), find the value of \(\tan \theta\).
  2. A ladder 5 m long, leans against a vertical wall at an angle of \(70^\circ\) to the ground. The ladder slips down the wall 2 m. Find, correct to two significant figures,
    1. the new angle which the ladder makes with the ground;
    2. the distance the ladder slipped back on the ground from ts original position.
