DELTA INTELLECT

SSSCE 1999

Question 1:

  1. Consider the following two statements:

    \(P:\) All students are hardworking

    \(Q:\) No hardworking person is careless

    1. Draw a Venn diagram to illustrate the above statements.
    2. Which of the following statements are valid or not valid conclusions from \(P\) and \(Q\)?

\(\alpha\)) Ama is a student \(\Rightarrow\) Ama is not careless.

\(\beta\)) Kwame is hardworking \(\Rightarrow\) Kwame is a student.

\(\gamma\)) Esi is careless \(\Rightarrow\) Esi is not a student.

  1. \(A=\{ 1,2,3,4,6 \}\) and \(B=\{ 1,2,3,6,9,18 \}\) are subsets of the universal set \(\mu =\{ 1,2,3,4,6,9,12,18,36 \}\). List the elements of
    1. \(A^\prime \cap B\)
    2. \((A^\prime \cap B)^\prime\)

Question 2:

  1. If \(\displaystyle\frac{3m-n}{5m-n}=\displaystyle\frac{p}{q}\), express \(m\) in terms of \(p,q\) and \(n\).

  2. Without using tables and calculator, express \(\displaystyle\frac{(0.00042\times 10^{-8})(15,000)}{(5000\times 10^7)(0.0021\times 10^{14})}\) in the form \(a \times 10^n\), where \(1<a<10\) and \(n\) is an integer.

Question 3:

  1. Copy and complete the multiplication, \(\otimes\), table for modulo 8 on the set \(\{ 2,3,4,5,6,7 \}\).
\(\otimes\) 2 3 4 5 6 7
2 4 6 0 2 4 6
3 6 4 2 3
4 0 4 4 0 4
5 7 4 1 3
6 4 2 0 6 4
7 6 4 2 1
  1. From your table, find
    1. the truth set of the equation \(n \otimes n=1\)
    2. the value of \((3 \otimes 5)\otimes 6\)

Question 4:

\(A(-2,3), B(2, -1),C(5, 0)\) and \(D(x, y)\) are the vertices of the parallelogram \(ABCD\).

  1. Find \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{DC}\). Hence, find the coordinates of \(D\).
  2. Calculate, correct to one decimal place \(|\overrightarrow{DB}|\).

Question 5:

Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only, construct

  1. triangle \(ABC\) with \(|AB|=6\) cm, \(|BC|=8\) cm and \(\angle ABC=90^\circ\).
  2. a point \(D\) on \(AC\), which is equidistant from \(B\) and \(C\).
  3. Measure \(|BD|\)

Question 6:

  1. Simplify \(3\sqrt{27}-2\sqrt{3}(4\sqrt{3}-5\sqrt{12})\), leaving your answer in surd form.
  2. A variable \(y\) is partly constant and partly varies as the square of \(x\). When \(x=2, y=6\) and when \(x=3, y=10\).
    1. Find the equation connecting \(x\) and \(y\).
    2. Find the values of \(x\) when \(y=5\).
    3. Find the values of \(y\) when \(x=4\).

Question 7:

  1. In the diagram(not drawn to scale), \(ABCD\) is a quadrilateral \(\angle ABC=\angle ACD=90^\circ, |AB|=3\) cm, \(|BC|=4\) cm and \(|AD|=13\) cm. Find the area of the quadrilateral.

  1. In the diagram(not drawn to scale), \(AB\) is a diameter if the circle \(ABCD\). \(DC\) is parallel to \(AB\) and \(\angle BAC=25^\circ\). Calculate
    1. \(\angle ADC\)
    2. \(\angle CAD\)

Question 8:

In a certain country, the annual income tax payable by an individual is as follows

Amount(in cedis) Rate of Tax
First 140,700 Free Tax
" 100,000 5%
" 150,000 15%
" 200,000 25%
" 300,000 35%
" 350,000 45%
" 400,000 55%

Mr. Hermosa’s annual salary is 1,390,700.00 cedis. Calculate:

  1. his taxable income
  2. his annual income
  3. the percentage of his income that went into tax, correct to two significant figures.

Question 9:

  1. Copy and complete the table of values for the relation \(y=x^2-2x-3\) for the interval \(-2 \leq x \leq 4\).
\(x\) \(-2\) \(-1.5\) \(-1\) \(0\) \(1\) \(2\) \(2.5\) \(3\) \(3.5\) \(4\)
\(y\) \(5\) \(-1\) \(0\)
  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 1 unit on both axes, draw the graph of of the relation \(y=x^2-2x-3\) for the given interval.
  2. Use your graph to find
    1. the solution of \(x^2-2x-2=0\);
    2. the equation of the line of symmetry;
    3. the range of values of \(x\) for which \(y\) is negative.

Question 10:

\(A, B, X\) and \(Y\) are four points in a horizontal plane. \(B\) is on a bearing of \(090^\circ\) from \(A\). \(X\) is 7.5 m due north of \(B\) and on a bearing of \(052^\circ\) from \(A\). \(Y\) is due north of \(A\) and on a bearing of \(340\) from \(B\). calculate correct to three significant figures:

  1. \(|AB|\)
  2. \(|AY|\)
  3. the components of \(\overrightarrow{XY}\)
  4. the distance and bearing of \(Y\) from \(X\).

Question 11:

The table below shows the height in millimetres of a sample of 250 seedlings on an experimental farm.

Height of seedlings(mm) \(0-4\) \(5-9\) \(10-14\) \(15-19\) \(20-24\) \(25-29\) \(30-34\) \(35-39\)
No. of seedlings 40 45 60 40 30 21 10 5
  1. Construct a cumulative frequency table and use it to draw a cumulative frequency curve for the sample.
  2. Using your cumulative frequency curve, find
    1. the first and third quartiles
    2. the percentage of the sample of seedlings whose heights are above 22 mm.

Question 12:

The diagram shows a hut made if a onical roof and a cylindrical wall. The height of the roof is 2 m and its base is 3.5 m. The cylindrical wall has a height of 2.5 m and base area of 28.26 m\(^2\).

  1. calculate, correct to three significant figures,
    1. the slant height of the conical roof
    2. the surface area of the conical roof
    3. the external surface area of the wall.
  2. If the cost of painting a square metre surface is 450.00 cedis, calculate the total cost of painting the outer area of the wall and the roof of the hut. [Take \(\pi\) = 3.14]
