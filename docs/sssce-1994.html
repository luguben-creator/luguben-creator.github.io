Question 1:

Without using calculator,

  1. evaluate \(\displaystyle\frac{2x-y}{z}+\displaystyle\frac{z+2y}{x}\), when \(x=2, y=-3\) and \(z=4\).

  2. find \(Q\) if \(3Q+13^2=16^2\)

    2. Use the table to find the truth set of \(n\otimes n\otimes 6=5\).

Question 3:

  1. The universal set \(U=\{2, 3, 5, 7\}, P=\{2, 5\}\) and \(Q=\{5, 7\}\). Find
    1. \((P\cap U)^\prime\)
    2. \(P^\prime \cup Q^\prime\)

    State the relationship between i. and ii.

  2. In a class of 50 students, 30 offer Economics, 17 offer government and 7 offer neither Economics nor Government. How many students offer both subjects?

Question 4:

  1. Evaluate \(\sqrt{7}\left(3\sqrt{7}+\displaystyle\frac{6}{\sqrt{7}} \right)\).

  2. Find the truth set of the simultaneous equation. \(\displaystyle\frac{5}{6}x-\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}y=2; \, \displaystyle\frac{1}{2}x-\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}y=\displaystyle\frac{5}{2}\)

Question 5:

\(X, Y\) and \(Z\) are such that \(X\) varies directly as \(Z\) and inversely as the cube root of \(Y\). If \(X=8, Y=27\) and \(Z=4\), find

  1. an expression for \(X\) in terms of \(Y\) and \(Z\).
  2. the value of \(Y\) when \(X=12\) and \(Z=10\).

Question 6:

\(OABCD\) is a right square pyramid with vertex \(O\), such that \(|OA|=|OB|=|OC|=|OD|=30\) m and \(|AB|=25\) m. Calculate:

  1. the height of the pyramid
  2. the volume of the pyramid
  3. the angle between \(OA\) and \(AC\).
  4. the total surface area of the pyramid (excluding the base).

Question 8:

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on both axes, draw on a sheet of graph paper two perpendicular axes, \(Ox\) and \(Oy\) for the intervals \(-10\le x \le 10\) and \(-10\le y \le 10\).

  2. Draw on the same graph sheet, clearly labelling the vertices and indicating the co-ordinates,

    1. triangle \(PQR\) with \(P(2, 2),\overrightarrow{PQ}=\begin{pmatrix} 2\\4\end{pmatrix}\) and \(\overrightarrow{QR}=\begin{pmatrix} 2\\-4\end{pmatrix}\).

    2. the image triangle \(P^\prime Q^\prime R^\prime\) of triangle \(PQR\) under a reflection in the line \(x=0\), where \(P\to P^\prime, Q\to Q^\prime\) and \(R\to R^\prime\).

    3. the image of \(P^{\prime\prime} Q^{\prime\prime} R^{\prime\prime}\) of triangle \(PQR\) under a rotation of \(180^\circ\) about the origin, where \(P\to P^{\prime\prime}, Q\to Q^{\prime\prime}\) and \(R\to R^{\prime\prime}\).

    1. Describe the single transformation that maps triangle \(P^\prime Q^\prime R^\prime\) onto the triangle \(P^{\prime\prime} Q^{\prime\prime} R^{\prime\prime}\) where \(P^\prime\to P^{\prime\prime}, Q^\prime\to Q^{\prime\prime}\) and \(R^\prime \to R^{\prime\prime}\).

    2. Find \(\overrightarrow{P^\prime R^\prime}\).

Question 9:

Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only,

  1. construct \(\bigtriangleup PRQ\) such that \(|PQ|=9\) cm, \(\angle PQR=75^\circ\) and \(\angle QPR=60^\circ\).
  2. locate a point \(T\), inside \(\bigtriangleup PRQ\) such that it is equidistant from \(\overline{RQ}, \overline{RP}\) and \(\overline{PQ}\).
  3. construct the circle which touches the three sides of \(\bigtriangleup PRQ\) and measure its radius.

Question 10:

Kofi and Yaw entered into a business partnership with a total capital of 81 million cedis. They are agreed to contribute the capital in the ratio 2:1 respectively. The profit was shared as follows:

Kofi was paid \(5\%\) of the total profit for his service as the manager. Each partner was paid \(3\%\) of the capital invested. The remainder of the profit was then shared between them in the ratio of their contribution to the capital. If Kofi’s share of the total profit was 7.5 million cedis, calculate

  1. the total profit for the year to the nearest thousand cedis.
  2. Yaw’s share of the profit as a percentage of his contribution to the capital.

Question 11:

  1. Copy and complete the following table for the relation \(y=3+2x-x^2\) for \(-2\le x \le 4\).
\(x\) \(-2\) \(-1\) \(0\) \(1\) \(1.5\) \(2\) \(2.5\) \(3\) \(3.5\) \(4\)
\(y\) \(0\) \(3\) \(1.75\) \(-5\)
  1. Taking 2 cm as 1 unit on both axes, draw the graph of the relation for the given interval.
  2. Draw on the same axes the graph of \(x-y=0\).
  3. Using your graphs,
    1. solve the equation \(3+2x-x^2=x\).
    2. find the value of \(x\) for which \(3+2x-x^2=2\).

Question 12:

  1. A village \(P\) is 10 km from a lorry station, \(Q\) on a bearing of \(065^\circ\). Another village \(R\) is 8 km from \(Q\) on a bearing of \(155^\circ\), calculate:
    1. the distance of \(R\) from \(P\) to the nearest kilometre.
    2. the bearing of \(R\) from \(P\) to the nearest degree.
  2. \(M\) is a village on \(\overline{PR}\) such that \(\overline{QM}\) is perpendicular to \(\overline{PR}\). Find the distance of \(M\) from \(P\) to the nearest kilometre.
