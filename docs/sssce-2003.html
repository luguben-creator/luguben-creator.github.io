DELTA INTELLECT

SSSCE 2003

Question 1:

  1. Without using mathematical tables or calculators, evaluate \(\displaystyle\frac{0.0125 \times 0.00576}{0.0015 \times 0.32}\) leaving your answer in standard form.(All working must be shown including rough work).

    1. Simplify \(2 \sqrt{3} (2 - \sqrt{3}) + 3 \sqrt{2} (\sqrt{2} - 1)\).
    2. Hence, evaluate your answer in (i) correct to 2 decimal places given that \(\sqrt{2}=1.414\) and \(\sqrt{3}=1.732\)

Question 2:

\(A = \left \{ x: \displaystyle\frac{2x-1}{3} < \displaystyle\frac{x+3}{5} \right\}\),

\(B = \left\{ x: \displaystyle\frac{2x}{3} - 5 < 3x+2 \right\}\)

  1. Find \(A \cap B\)
  2. Illustrate your answer in (a) on the number line.

Question 3:

  1. A trader sold 1,750 articles for 525,000.00 cedis and made a profit of \(20\%\).
    1. Calculate the cost price of each article.
    2. If he wanted \(45\%\) profit on the cost price, how much should he have sold each of the articles?
  2. Factorize completely \(2xy-6mn-3my+4nx\).

Question 4:

\(P\) varies inversely as the square of \((Q+1)\) and \(P\) is 2 when \(Q\) is 3.

  1. Write an equation connecting \(P\) and \(Q\).
  2. Find the possible values of \(Q\), when \(P=8\).

Question 5:

Two vectors \(\textbf{p}\) and \(\textbf{q}\) are defined by \(\textbf{p} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \cos x \\ 5 \sin x \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\textbf{q} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \cos x \\ 2 \sin x \end{pmatrix}\).

  1. If \(\textbf{p} + \textbf{q} = \begin{pmatrix} 4.690 \\ 5.208 \end{pmatrix}\), find to the nearest whole number the value of \(x\), where \(x\) is acute.
  2. Find the value of vector \(\textbf{c}\), if \(\textbf{c} = 2 \textbf{p} + \textbf{q}\).

Question 6:

100 members of a community were asked to state the activities they undertake during the day.

38 go to school
54 go to fishing
50 engage in trading
10 go to School and also Fish
18 go to School and also Trade
22 go for Fishing and also Trade

Each of these members undertakes at least one of the activities. The number of people who go to School only is the same as the number who engage in Trading only. Use the information to find the number of people who

  1. undertake all the three activities;
  2. go to School only.

Question 7:

The marks scored by 15 pupils in a test are as follows:

14, 14, 11, 13, 17, 14, 11, 13, 20, 19, 17, 11, 20, 14, 17.

Find

  1. the mean mark;
  2. the standard deviation, correct to 2 decimal places.

Question 8:

  1. Copy and complete the table of values for the relation \(y = \displaystyle\frac{10}{x+3} +3x - 3\) for the interval \(-2.25 \leq x \leq 1.5\).
\(x\) \(-2.25\) \(-2.0\) \(-1.5\) \(-1.0\) \(-0.5\) \(0\) \(0.5\) \(1.0\) \(1.5\)
\(y\) \(3.58\) \(-1.0\) \(-0.5\) \(1.36\) \(2.5\)
  1. Using a scale of 4 cm to 1 unit on the \(x\)-axis and 2 cm to 1 unit on the \(y\)-axis, draw a graph for the relation.
  2. Estimate from your graph,
    1. the solution of the equation \((3x-3)(3x+3)+10=0\),
    2. the values of \(x\) for which \(y=1\).

Question 9:

  1. Solve for \(x\) if \(\log_{10} (8x+1) - \log_{10} (2x+1) = \log_{10} (x+2)\).

  2. In the diagram, \(P,Q,R\) and \(S\) are points on a circle. \(|PQ|=|PS|, \angle PQS = 40^\circ\) and \(\angle QSR=25^\circ\). Calculate the value of
    1. \(\angle QPS\);
    2. \(\angle QRS\);
    3. \(\angle RQS\).

Question 10:

  1. The diagonals of a rhombus are 8 cm and 10 cm long. Calculate correct to 1 decimal place, the length of a side of the rhombus.
  2. A hollow right circular cone stands with its base on a horizontal table. It is 100 cm high with a base radius of 20 cm. It is filled with water through the vertex upto a depth of 25 cm. Calculate,
    1. the radius of the circle formed by the water surface;
    2. correct to the nearest whole number, the volume of water inside the cone. \(\left[ \text{Take } \pi = \frac{22}{7} \right]\)

Question 11:

  1. Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on both axes, draw on a sheet of graph paper two perpendicular axes \(Ox\) and \(Oy\) for the intervals \(-10 \leq x \leq 10\) and \(-10 \leq y \leq 10\).
  2. Draw on the same graph sheet indicating clearly the coordinates of all vertices,
    1. \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) with vertices \(P(6, 9), Q(4, 4)\) and \(R(9, 6)\).
    2. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_1 Q_1 R_1\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under a reflection in the line \(y=1\), where \(P \to P_1, Q \to Q_1\) and \(R \to R_1\).
    3. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_2 Q_2 R_2\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under an anticlockwise rotation of \(90^\circ\) about the origin, where \(P \to P_2, Q \to Q_2\) and \(R \to R_2\).
    4. the image \(\bigtriangleup P_3 Q_3 R_3\) of \(\bigtriangleup PQR\) under a translation by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} -9\\-9 \end{pmatrix}\) where \(P \to P_3, Q \to Q_3\) and \(R \to R_3\).
  3. Find the gradient of the line \(P_3 Q\).

Question 12:

  1. A ship leaves a point \(T\) and sails on a bearing \(030^\circ\) to a point \(P\), 15 km away. It then sails on a bearing of \(120^\circ\) to a point \(Q\), 20 km away from \(P\). Calculate \(|TQ|\).
    1. Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only, construct a quadrilateral \(ABCD\) with \(|AB|=9\) cm, \(|BC|=10\) cm, \(|AD|=7.5\) cm, \(\angle ABC = 45^\circ\) and \(\angle BAD=135^\circ\).
    2. What type of quadrilateral is \(ABCD\)?
